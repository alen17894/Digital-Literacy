# Stay Safe Online: Cybersecurity Checklist for College Students

Here are specific, actionable steps to protect your digital identity and finances while navigating college life in India.

**Financial & UPI Safety:**
1. **Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money:** Remember that your UPI PIN is only required to *deduct* money from your account, never to receive it.
2. **Verify VPAs and QR codes:** Before authorizing a payment at the campus canteen or local shops, double-check the receiver's name that pops up on your app. Scammers often replace legitimate QR codes with their own.
3. **Beware of fake internship fees:** Never pay an upfront "registration" or "kit fee" for a part-time job or internship. Legitimate employers will never ask you to pay to work.

**General Digital Hygiene:**
4. **Avoid public Wi-Fi for transactions:** Do not use open college or cafe Wi-Fi for logging into bank accounts or making fee payments. Switch to your mobile data.
5. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Turn on 2FA for all critical accounts, especially your university email, WhatsApp, and Instagram. 
6. **Limit location sharing on social media:** Avoid posting real-time updates of your whereabouts, class schedules, or hostel location on public stories to prevent stalking and social engineering.
7. **Use strong, unique passwords:** Avoid using your birth date, phone number, or pet's name. Use a password manager to keep track of distinct passwords for different university portals.
8. **Keep software updated:** Regularly update your phone and laptop operating systems and apps. These updates often contain critical patches for newly discovered security vulnerabilities.
