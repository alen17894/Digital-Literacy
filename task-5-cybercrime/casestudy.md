# Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a cybercrime where scammers socially engineer victims into authorizing direct, unauthorized money transfers from their bank accounts. It exploits the victim's lack of familiarity with the specific mechanics of digital payment gateways.

In a realistic scenario, a student lists a used laptop for sale on a local online marketplace. A fraudster contacts the student, feigning urgent interest and agreeing to the asking price. To "secure" the purchase, the scammer insists on sending an immediate advance payment via a UPI app. 

Here is how the crime typically unfolds step-by-step:
1. **The Hook:** The scammer sends a custom QR code or a payment link directly to the victim's messaging app.
2. **The Deception:** The scammer calls the victim, instructing them to scan the code or click the link to "receive" the funds.
3. **The Trap:** The link actually triggers a "Request Money" prompt on the victim's UPI app, rather than a deposit notification. 
4. **The Execution:** The scammer rushes the victim, claiming that entering their secret UPI PIN is a mandatory security step to accept the incoming money. Once the victim inputs the PIN, funds are instantly deducted from their own account and routed to the scammer.

This crime frequently targets people selling items online, the elderly, or individuals newly adopting digital payments. The consequences are immediate and severe, primarily involving irreversible financial loss, as UPI transfers are instantaneous. Secondary consequences include significant psychological distress and a lingering loss of trust in digital banking infrastructure.
