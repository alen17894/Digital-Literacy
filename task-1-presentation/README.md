Project Report: Task 1  
Tool Used: Canva  

What the Design Shows:  
My design is a single-slide visual guide to help my classmates understand the digital landscape. I centered the slide around three main pillars. First, it defines digital literacy and emphasizes that it includes more than just basic computer skills. It also involves evaluating and communicating information responsibly. Second, it highlights important digital tools for students, grouping them into cloud storage, productivity, and design apps to show how technology supports various academic workflows. Finally, it outlines key safe internet practices and offers practical tips on creating strong passwords, recognizing phishing attempts, and managing one’s digital footprint.

Reflection:  
One challenging part of creating this presentation was breaking down complex information into easy-to-digest, visually appealing segments. While using my Acer Predator laptop, I initially relied on dense blocks of text. I quickly realized that as a Digital Ambassador, my goal is to ensure readability. Striking a balance between concise bullet points, relevant icons, distinct color coding, and enough whitespace to keep viewers engaged without creating visual clutter was tough, but it felt incredibly rewarding to get the layout just right.
